PS2 — Event-Driven Congestion · Exploratory Data Analysis¶

Flipkart × Bengaluru Traffic Police. This notebook explores the provided incident dataset only (no external data) to ground the modelling of three targets — requires_road_closure (T1), priority (T2) and resolution_time (T3) — and surfaces the three data findings that shaped every downstream decision:

  1. End-point coordinates leak the closure label (recorded because a closure happened).
  2. Priority is a deterministic function of corridor — a rule, not a prediction.
  3. Resolution time is partly a recording artifact + a multi-week civic-process tail.

Closure is overwhelmingly event-driven: VIP movement, public events, protests, tree-fall and construction close roads far more than breakdowns/accidents. Planned events close roads ~5× as often as unplanned — exactly the PS focus.

7. Free-text officer notes¶

~83% of incidents carry a short note (English/Kanglish ~87%, Kannada ~13%). These are mined into structured features (severity, lane-blocking, tow-needed, subtype) via a local multilingual LLM (qwen2.5:3b) with a fast rule-based fallback.

8. Takeaways → modelling¶

  • T1: learnable; ban end-point features; key signals = event_cause, is_planned, corridor recurrence, LLM severity. Use PR-AUC + calibrated probabilities.
  • T2: present honestly as the corridor rule (+ a corridor-blind sanity model).
  • T3: artifact-limited; use AFT survival (uses censored 'active' incidents) for ranking + a 6h-capped regressor for point estimates; report robust metrics and the data-quality recommendation.
  • Splitting: strictly chronological; recurrence features are past-only.
